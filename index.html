Aim Training Game

Aim Training Game

A game for the computer, programed in C++ with the library SFML (Simple and Fast Multimedia Library).

In this game you destroy planets by clicking on them with your mouse. When starting the game the mouse cursor will be hidden, instead there's a crosshair in the middle of the screen and when moving the mouse the background and the planets move while the crosshair stays in the middle of the screen. That way it creates the illusion of being able to look around in the game.

Virtual Chessboard

Virtual Chessboard

Software that acts as a virtual chessboard. This isn't really supposed to be a chess game to play against someone with.

I made This so I could replay chess positions i saw in a match, or encountered in my own games. Of course you can also play chess against a friend with it if you're traveling and didn't have space for a chessboard and chess pieces. Another use could be for example, you're playing with someone via chats, email or other types of messages, so you send and recive the moves in written form like "e4, e5". In that case you can now replay the moves in the virtual chessboard and take a look at, it instead of having to visualize it in your head.
